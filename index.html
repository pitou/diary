One way to visualize
a personal diary.
Intro
On August 10, 2021, I reached the last page of my diary, which I started writing on the first day of 2020.
I thought it would be interesting to try to represent the structure of the pages using simple design choices.
The rules are:
  • each section inside a page represents a single writing session, on a certain date
  • the height of each section is proportional to the actual space that it occupies on the original page
  • if a writing session spans over multiple pages, the upper and/or lower side of a single section will be a dashed line, to express continuation
  • if I skipped writing for one or more days, at the top of the section you will see a colored block; the darker the color, the more days were skipped

I'm thinking about an alternative view in which the number of rows in a section is taken in consideration instead of its height; sooner or later I will implement it. Being a quick experiment made in a few days, honestly I do not expect this project to grow further than that, but who knows.
Anyway, thank you for visiting! See you in the future for the analysis of volume two :)
